The Difference Between Being Alone and Loneliness

Vladimir Poplavskij
12/29/2024
loneliness solitude mental-health well-being self-improvement personal-growth isolation self-reflection happiness mindfulness
This blog post explores the difference between being alone and feeling lonely. It discusses how to embrace solitude, turn loneliness into a positive experience, and offers practical tips for personal growth during times of isolation.

Social isolation can affect your mental health if you are not used to spending time with yourself. However, even this can be adapted to and enjoyed.

Many thinkers over the centuries have emphasized the importance of coming to terms with this state. Michel de Montaigne once even wrote: "The greatest thing in the world is to know how to belong to yourself."

Solitude encourages the imagination and makes it possible for serious thoughts to appear in people's heads that go beyond the usual routine and force the convolutions to move.

Loneliness, on the other hand, is a negative experience. Self-pity, thoughts of a wasted life, sadness torment. And if you are really interested in your self-development, then it does not hurt to turn this feeling into positive and constructive.

The first step to being happy alone with yourself is accepting the situation. You need to ask yourself questions, turning loneliness into a constructive pastime: what do I really value in life? What do I need most? What do I need to do with...? What's next?

The second step is to keep your mind and body in constant tone. You need your body to calm your brain. You need feelings to organize your thoughts. Do not feed yourself with empty chatter and empty dramatic thoughts that only worsen the situation.

You don't have to run from anything. Instead of running away from what is, you should notice what is right under your nose. It sounds a bit complicated, but think about it.

Try starting a journal and writing down thoughts about what interests you. Write poetry. Download Duolingo and delve into some new language. Start writing your autobiography or a book. Visit a virtual museum. Learn something new through YouTube or take a course that will help accelerate your career growth and expand your competence. Anything.

Being happy alone with yourself is a constant striving to be better mentally, socially and emotionally for yourself and your loved ones.

Today's topic is a bit complicated, but relevant - no offense. And we will end it with a wise saying: "You cannot be lonely if you like the person you are alone with."
